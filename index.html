Unity Training Module Testing

Max Dudek, Alex Chlpka, Aaron Reuter

Conjecture

Unity is a powerful but complicated development tool with a steep learning curve. Proficiency in Unity comes easier for those with a strong background in computer science, but it can be a very powerful framework for anyone looking to create an interactive environment. Our goal is to provide feedback on Open Lab's Unity Training Module to broaden the accessibility of Unity beyond experienced programmers.

Definition

We will be completing the Unity Training Module on Canvas, and building the example project that they walk through. We will write a report to Open Lab of our experience with the training, giving advice on how it can be clearer and more robust.

Quality

The goal of Open Lab's training modules are to make powerful development tools like Unity accessible to everyone, regardless of their computer science background. A game engine like Unity can be effective for technical communication by providing a means to construct interactive applications like infographics, informative video games, or tech demos.

Policy

After the Open Lab demo, we will explore Unity on our own to see what we can create based on our experience from the training module. Our experimentation will inform our presentation to the class, where we will explain our learning process, and how we believe Unity can be used for technical communication.
